Abstract

Federated Learning (FL) enables collaborative training without exposing raw data, yet remains vulnerable to gradient inversion attacks that recover private inputs from shared gradients. We present GI-DQA, the first attack that successfully reconstructs sensitive document question-answering inputs by combining optimization-based image recovery with analytic extraction of question–answer pairs. GI-DQA recovers >70 % of document words on state-of-the-art multimodal models, revealing new privacy risks and motivating stronger defenses.

Key Contributions

  • Introduces GI-DQA — the first gradient inversion attack targeting multimodal Document QA models.
  • Hybrid strategy: visual pixel optimization + analytic question–answer reconstruction.
  • Novel loss with gradient matching (MSE + cosine) and text-aware priors (Laplacian, Gaussian, Spatial & Channel TV).
  • Comprehensive evaluation on Donut-Base and LayoutLMv3-Base using the privacy-aware PFL-DocVQA dataset.
  • Proposes Safe Template — an input-level defense that drops attack success from 70 % to <1 % without hurting utility.

Method Overview

Hybrid Reconstruction Pipeline

GI-DQA takes leaked gradients (∇θ), public document templates, and model weights as input. It:

  1. Extracts the exact question & answer tokens analytically.
  2. Optimizes document pixels to match client gradients while respecting visual priors.
  3. Iteratively refines until gradients align and priors converge.

Loss Components

  • Gradient Match = MSE + Cosine Similarity (layer-wise).
  • Laplacian Text Prior (Rtxt) — sharpens word edges.
  • Gaussian Prior (Rgau) — removes high-frequency noise.
  • Total Variation (RTV-S, RTV-C) — enforces spatial & channel smoothness.
  • Exponential Scheduler φ(t) — high prior weight early, decays over iterations.

Safe Template Defense

Organizations can embed tiny, human-imperceptible perturbations into public templates. Our Safe Template defense maximizes gradient divergence, blocking GI-DQA without changing document utility.

  • Random Noise: Works with ϵ > 32/255.
  • PGD Perturbations: Comparable protection at ϵ > 8/255.
  • No modifications to model or training loop; applied offline.
  • Reduces attack success to < 1 % on Donut-Base.
